FOUNDATIONS LEARNING PLAN

LONG TERM GOAL

Over the past 8 years I’ve worked in the services industry, mostly in sales and customer service. It’s helped me to gain good interpersonal skills which I believe will assist me in reaching an overall career commitment. At this stage, I know that I want to be a programmer but long term, I am uncertain. I like the idea of DevOps, Product Owner or in general a leader in tech. I think that some of the experiences through EDA will help me to realise what this may be but for now, my BFHG is to become a JavaScript programmer.

WHAT NON-TECHNICAL SKILLS WOULD YOU LIKE TO SEE DEVELOPED WITH EDA?

I would like to learn more about time management and overcoming imposter syndrome. I often have that inner voice that creeps into your mind and tells me I’m not good enough. Some tools to overcome this would be awesome.

STRENGTHS

  • Empathy. I try to understand other perspectives as much as possible. It has helped me to build relationships in the past.
  • Resilience. I work hard to overcome obstacles and reach an end point.
  • Honesty. I try my best to tell the truth, even if it will get myself into trouble.
  • Communication. I’m good with people and not afraid of having a conversation.
  • Fun. I love laughing!

LIMITATIONS

  • Asking for help. I struggle with asking for help sometimes because I am afraid of judgement.
  • Indecisiveness. I can get caught up in the details of a situation which can prevent me from making decisions.
  • Saying yes too much. I’ll commit to help someone purely out of politeness rather than actually looking at my calendar to check my availability.
  • Fear of failure. This has often stopped me from giving something a go.

HOW WILL YOU OVERCOME YOUR LIMITATIONS?

I pledge to ask for help either to my cohort members or facilitators after I have searched on Google and StackOverflow in a timely manner. I will also break down sprints into manageable timeframes and stop listening to the inner voice that tells me I'm not going to make it.

WHAT DO YOU EXPECT FROM THE FACILITATION TEAM?

I expect to be treated fairly and with honesty. I want to be better on a personal and professional level, the only way I can do that is by knowing and understanding myself. So, if I'm not dilivering or I'm not doing to something right, I want to be told so I can improve. Lastly, I gave up a career and a life to be here in this course, as I'm sure a lot of other people have, so the main thing is support.

BOOTCAMP LEARNING PLAN

WHAT IS YOUR LONG-TERM GOAL AND/OR CAREER PATHWAY?

My end goal from EDA is to finish with the skills that will enable me to gain employment as a full stack developer. I would like to use these skills in order to add value to the culture and technical performance of a company which aligns with what I am passionate about, helping people. In terms of my career path, I would like to continue growing my skills in order to reach a position as a software developer.

WHAT DO YOU THINK YOUR BIGGEST STRENGTHS AND LIMITATIONS WILL BE IN BOOTCAMP?

Originally, I thought that I would have some good technical abilities before going in but now I realize now that I currently lack the technical depth and problem-solving abilities that would assist me in becoming a good full stack developer. I also noticed I need to seriously improve on my organizational skills, create some better habits and practice discipline. This is something I will need to improve on and will hopefully get better with time. I also struggle with asking for help, I’m not a big fan of the spotlight and attention. This is also something I also need to get over.

I still believe I have good communicative and empathetic abilities which enables me to build good relationships. I never give up until a conclusion is met and will work hard to achieve results. I realized that I am quite detailed, which can be a positive and a negative. I focus on making things look good before they are committed, this includes making my code look organized before commits. I also am a sucker for consistent indentation, name schemes and avoiding deep nesting.

I will do my absolute best to not let my limitations hinder anyone else’s progress. If I am proving to be an obstacle, I would like to be told so I can work towards bettering myself.

WHAT DO YOU THINK YOUR BIGGEST NON-TECHNICAL CHALLENGE AT BOOTCAMP WILL BE?

I think that my biggest non-technical challenge will be discipline with staying on task 100% of the time and secondly my organizational skills in general. I believe it will help me, not only in my professional life but also my personal life as well. I would also like to mention speaking in front of a group – I’m not a huge fan of the spotlight.

WHAT NON-TECHNICAL SKILLS -HUMAN SKILLS- WOULD YOU LIKE TO SEE DEVELOPED IN YOURSELF WHILE AT EDA?

The main three things I would like to take away from EDA is a better grasp on time management, experience in pair coding and reflective skills.

WHAT ARE MY EXPECTATIONS FROM THE BOOTCAMP TEAM?

In order for me to have a successful experience with EDA I can’t stress enough how much I want the Bootcamp team to be 100% honest with me. In order to be a full stack developer, I need to know what I need to improve on, even at the risk of offending. Other than that, just being there to help at the appropriate time.

WHAT ARE YOUR EXPECTATIONS OF YOURSELF ON BOOTCAMP?

I declare that I will endeavor to seek a solution to problem via Google, Stack Overflow or various online platforms first, secondly through my cohort and lastly through my facilitators in a timely manner which will not impede my own or anyone else’s learning experience. I would like to take as much away from EDA as I possibly can in order to reach my goals.
